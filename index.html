Simple Omelette Recipe

An easy and quick dish, perfect for any meal. This classic omelette combines beaten eggs cooked to perfection, optionally filled with your choice of cheese, vegetables, or meats.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 10 minutes
  • Preparation: 5 minutes
  • Cooking: 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of butter or oil
  • Optional fillings: cheese, diced vegetables, cooked meats, herbs

Instructions

  1. Beat the eggs: In a bowl, beat the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper until they are well mixed. You can add a tablespoon of water or milk for a fluffier texture.
  2. Heat the pan: Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add butter or oil.
  3. Cook the omelette: Once the butter is melted and bubbling, pour in the eggs. Tilt the pan to ensure the eggs evenly coat the surface.
  4. Add fillings (optional): When the eggs begin to set at the edges but are still slightly runny in the middle, sprinkle your chosen fillings over one half of the omelette.
  5. Fold and serve: As the omelette continues to cook, carefully lift one edge and fold it over the fillings. Let it cook for another minute, then slide it onto a plate.
  6. Enjoy: Serve hot, with additional salt and pepper if needed.

Shiro

Shiro is a flavorful Ethiopian chickpea stew, smooth and creamy, often spiced with berbere and served with injera.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 40 minutes
  • Preparation: 10 minutes
  • Cooking: ~20-30 minutes

Ingredients

  • Chickpea flour (shiro powder)
  • Berbere spice mix & Salt
  • Onion & garlic
  • Oil or spiced clarified butter (ንጥር ቅቤ)
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Sauté finely chopped onions in oil or niter kibbeh until soft.
  2. Add minced garlic and cook briefly.
  3. Stir in berbere and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Mix in shiro powder, stirring to combine.
  5. Gradually add water or broth, stirring to avoid lumps.
  6. Simmer on low heat until thick and smooth.
  7. Adjust salt and serve hot with injera.

Fir Fir

Fir-fir is a spicy Ethiopian dish made by mixing torn pieces of injera with seasoned stew, often flavored with berbere and niter kibbeh.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 30 minutes
  • Preparation: ~5-10 minutes
  • Cooking: ~10-15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Injera (torn into bite-sized pieces)
  • Berbere spice mix
  • Berbere spice mix
  • Onion & garlic
  • Salt
  • Tomato (optional)
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Sauté chopped onions in niter kibbeh or oil until soft.
  2. Add minced garlic and cook briefly.
  3. Stir in berbere (and tomato if using) and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add a splash of water or broth to make a sauce.
  5. Mix in torn injera pieces, coating them evenly.
  6. Cook on low heat until warmed through and flavors blend.
  7. Serve hot, often with extra injera on the side.
